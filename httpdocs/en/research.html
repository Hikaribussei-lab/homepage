High-Tc Superconductor SARPES STM study of metal XMCD Inverse Photoemission
We study the solid-state physics, such as high temperature superconductors, magnetic materials, and thin films, using Synchrotron Radiation. Our major experimental tools are angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES), spin-ARPES, x-ray magnetic circular dichroism (XMCD), scanning tunneling microscopy (STM), and so on.
High-Tc Superconductor
We study Bi-based and La-based cuprates using angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES).
We are based on the Beam-Line1, 9 at HiSOR and measure cuprates on a few meV scale using low-energy photons.
Spin-resolved ARPES

We can obtain a spin-splitted data using our spin-resolved ARPES instrument. We focus on effcets which are depend on spin polarization. We are based on the Hiroshima Synchrotron Radiation Center.

STM Study of Metal

We study low dimensional materials on a substrate using STM. Our two STM instruments cover a wide range of temperature.

XMCD

We study thin magnetic films unsing soft X-ray at the BL-14 of HiSOR. XMCD experiment can reveal various properties of magetism.

Inverse Photoemission

We can measure unoccupied states using inverse ARPES and obtatin full energy bands by combination of ARPES.

Single Metal

We focus on single metals using ARPES at the BL-1 of HiSOR. We also maintain the ARPES instruments.
